"The lawyer who does not use discovery procedure is in the position of a physician who treats a serious case without first using the X-ray." - George Ragland, Jr.
Depositions permit a party to preserve potential testimony for introduction at trial and serve as a tool for investigating evidence before trial. This oral questioning of a witness was originally the exception rather than the rule. It was only available if the witness was ancient or infirm or was about to set off on a voyage to sea. In 1938 the pretrial phase of litigation was reformed to mimic procedures in use at trial itself to improve the administration of justice. Oral testimony is now available at the speed of light via video, just like an X-ray.
