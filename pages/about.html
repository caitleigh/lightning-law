About

We created Lightning law to increase access to evidence, to make the search for justice faster and more efficient and to lighten the burden on our Courts. Lightning Law was created to solve the problems frequently encountered by attorneys and court reporters, such as, difficulty scheduling depositions, expensive travel, heavy boxes of paper exhibits and long delays in payment for appearances and transcripts. One day, we hope to delivery lightning fast remote testimony in the Courtroom and around the world.

Our Story

Lightning Law was born in Seattle in 2017 right here, on this conference room table, when one lawyer decided to save trees by using scanned exhibits instead of paper ones. That lawyer, and others present, were surprised to find that the deposition went much faster than normal. This is a picture of that deposition, showing an uncluttered nearly paper free conference room table during a “paperless deposition.”

Suddenly,traveling to depositions became a lot “lighter,” armed with thumb drives instead of bankers’ boxes.

But the system was still far from perfect, not only for lawyers but also for court reporters. Lawyers wanted to spend more time with their families and less time on the road traveling to obtain testimony. Court Reporters wanted to be paid not only for their appearances but for their transcripts. Everyone seemed to be worried about a pending court reporter shortage. Lightning law was created to deliver remote, travel free testimony in a platform that ensures prompt payment for transcripts to Court Reporter. We deliver free rough transcripts to free up Court Reporters to make more appearances and to give lawyers access to testimony as quickly as possible.

Our Goal

Our goal is to master the remote deposition so that we can deliver remote in trial testimony. The administration of justice is a search for the truth. We find that the typical jury only hears part of the truth. A jury will hear only the testimony that that attorneys can deliver during trial. Witnesses who are far away or too busy do not testify. We would like to change that by streaming testimony into courtrooms.

Along the way, we see ways to make a difference for all litigants.

Remote in Court testimony can deliver essential translators, can allow in Court appearances for litigants who cannot otherwise afford to be there and can effectively open the courthouse doors to those who might otherwise never “step foot” in it. More justice brings more light.
